About

I was born and raised in Colorado. In 2022, I graduated from the University of Colorado Boulder with a bachelor's degree in mathematics with a focus on computational mathematics and a minor in computer science.

After graduating, I spent a year working for PhET Interactive Simulations, an education non-profit housed in the University of Colorado's physics department. PhET's mission is to create free, interactive math and science simulations, optimized for web browsers, designed to engage students through an intuitive, game-like environment. My responsibilities at PhET included spearheading the development and maintenance of a web application used to translate PhET simulations from English into other languages. I also worked for Numberscope, a project housed in the University of Colorado's mathematics department. The project aims to be a research and citizen scientist tool to combine integer sequences (e.g. the Fibonacci sequence) with visualizers (e.g. turtle graphics).

In 2023, I started work at the Clinical Genome Resource (ClinGen) project housed in Stanford University's school of medicine. ClinGen is an initiative funded by the National Institutes of Health dedicated to constructing a central repository elucidating the clinical significance of genes and variants for precision medicine and research. In my role at ClinGen, I craft and maintain front-end and back-end features for ClinGen's gene curation interface and its variant curation interface.
