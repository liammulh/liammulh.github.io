John Ousterhout, a computer science professor at Stanford and (amongst other things) creator of Tcl and Tk, wrote a book expounding his software design philosophy. I provide a brief overview of the book before I give my impressions and takeaways.
Published: Thursday, January 1, 1970
Tagged under: software-engineering, design
[DRAFT]

TL;DR: An excellent book worth reading. It's The Elements of Style for software design.

The book covers a lot.

Most of the opinions in the book are uncontroversial. However, there are a few admonitions an engineer might reasonably quibble about.

I learned a lot from the book.
