About this blog and its author, Liam Mulhall

Hello and welcome! My name is Liam. I'm a software engineer based in the San Francisco Bay Area.

About this blog

I mostly write expository prose for my own edification. Sometimes I write about things I'm working on.

About me

My background

I was born and raised in Colorado's Roaring Fork Valley. I attended the University of Colorado Boulder, where in May of 2022 I obtained a bachelor's degree in mathematics with a specialization in computation, complemented by a minor in computer science.

My career thus far

I'm a software engineer working on the Clinical Genome Resource (ClinGen) project housed in Stanford University's school of medicine. ClinGen is an initiative funded by the National Institutes of Health dedicated to constructing a central repository elucidating the clinical significance of genes and variants for precision medicine and research. In my role at ClinGen, I craft and maintain front-end and back-end features for ClinGen's gene curation interface and its variant curation interface.

Before ClinGen, I worked at PhET Interactive Simulations, an education non-profit housed in the University of Colorado's physics department. PhET's mission is to create free, interactive math and science simulations, optimized for web browsers, designed to engage students through an intuitive, game-like environment. My responsibilities at PhET included spearheading the development and maintenance of a web application used to translate PhET simulations from English into other languages.

I've also worked for Numberscope, a software project housed in the University of Colorado Boulder's Experimental Mathematics Lab whose aim is to provide a browser-based tool for visualizing integer sequences like the Fibonacci sequence. My contributions included upgrading dependencies, adding tests, writing documentation, server administration, and fixing back-end bugs.

My project portfolio

In the summer of 2023, I began work on Underwood, a static blog generator initially conceived as a modest Bash script. It's now a Python package published on the Python Package Index (PyPI). This blog was generated using Underwood.

In the fall of 2022, I made 0ty, a static website generator leveraging Node.js. I finished working on it in the spring of 2023, and published it to the NPM registry. I abandoned it in favor of Underwood.

In the fall of 2018, I took a class on data structures at CU Boulder in which I wrote a neural network from scratch in C++ for my final project. This neural network was trained to behave like an exclusive or (XOR) gate.

My expertise

In obtaining my degree in pure mathematics, I learned how to approach abstract problems with creative yet methodical thinking. I also learned how to identify and exploit patterns in abstract problems. Both of these skills have proved invaluable across many domains.

As a software engineer, I've learned the art of decomposing complex problems into manageable components. I've also learned how to design abstractions, balance constraints, communicate effectively in writing and speech, and build team consensus.
