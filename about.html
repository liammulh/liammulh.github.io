About Liam Mulhall and his blog

Greetings and welcome to my humble digital abode. My name is Liam. I'm a software engineer based in the San Francisco Bay Area.

About this blog

I'm an ardent advocate of perpetual learning and a fervent scribbler. For me, expository writing clarifies concepts. This blog serves as a repository for my evolving knowledge and insights, where I expound upon my learning experiences and discoveries. Please bear in mind this blog is not intended to serve as an infallible fount of knowledge but rather as a dynamic space for intellectual exploration.

About me

My background

I was born and raised in the picturesque Roaring Fork Valley of Colorado. I attended the University of Colorado Boulder, where in May of 2022 I obtained a bachelor's degree in mathematics with a specialization in computation, complemented by a minor in computer science, a stepping stone to my current vocation.

My career thus far

In my current capacity, I am a software engineer working on the Clinical Genome Resource (ClinGen) project housed in Stanford University's school of medicine. ClinGen is an initiative funded by the National Institutes of Health (NIH) dedicated to constructing a central repository elucidating the clinical significance of genes and variants for precision medicine and research. In my role at ClinGen, I craft and maintaining both front-end and back-end features for ClinGen's gene curation interface and its variant curation interface, pivotal tools employed by genetics researchers to standardize the curation of genetic information.

Before ClinGen, I worked at PhET Interactive Simulations, an esteemed educational non-profit housed within the University of Colorado's physics department. PhET's mission revolves around the creation of free, interactive math and science simulations, optimized for web browsers, designed to engage students through an intuitive, game-like environment. My responsibilities at PhET included spearheading the development and maintenance of a web application used to translate PhET simulations from English into other languages.

I have also lent my expertise to Numberscope, a software project housed within the University of Colorado Boulder's Experimental Mathematics Lab whose aim is to provide a browser-based tool for visualizing integer sequences like the Fibonacci sequence. My contributions encompassed diverse facets, such as upgrading dependencies, conducting rigorous testing, crafting comprehensive documentation, and performing server administration, in addition to resolving back-end bugs.

My project portfolio

In the summer of 2023, I began work on Underwood, a static blog generator initially conceived as a modest Bash script but ultimately metamorphosed into a Python package that now graces the Python Package Index (PyPI). This very blog was generated using Underwood.

In the fall of 2022, I forged 0ty, a static website generator leveraging Node.js. Its development concluded in the spring of 2023, and it made its home on the NPM registry. I abandoned it in favor of Underwood.

In the fall of 2018, I took a class on data structures at CU Boulder where I ventured into the realm of C++ to construct a neural network from scratch. This neural network was trained to behave like an exclusive or (XOR) gate. The project files are up on GitHub.

My expertise

My pursuit of a degree in pure mathematics endowed me with the ability to approach abstract problems with creative yet methodical thinking. My proficiency in identifying patterns and applying logical rigor has proven universally applicable across diverse domains.

My affinity for writing, nurtured throughout my academic and professional career, has fortified my communication skills. The art of writing remains a cherished pursuit, one that I continually refine.

As a professional in software engineering, I've learned the art of decomposing complex problems into manageable components. My adeptness in designing abstractions, balancing constraints, and effective communication has been refined over years of practice.
